My Best Books Of 2024

Problem Power - Apeh Harrison Iwodi

Iwodi doesn't downplay the very real struggles we encounter. He acknowledges the pain and difficulty that comes with life's challenges. However, he argues that these problems are not unique to you. Millions of people face similar hurdles. This realization can be a powerful one. When you see your problems as part of a shared human experience, the burden can feel lighter.

The book aims to empower readers to shift their mindset from feeling like a victim of circumstance of little problmes like A crowded bus, a stain on your shirt,A dead phone battery and see it as just part of daily life

Why helping you is hurting me , Carmen Berry

In an attempt to hide trauma adults are driven to help others who remind them of themselves during there own time of pain . How can that be harmful, well it is an addiction nevertheless an addiction leads to lack of control of choices and feelings. Messiahs tend to give to others what they do desperately need to receive themselves. What others actually need goes unnoticed because the messiah sees other people’s pain and feels their own. Their wounds are in the tears of others , love is hardly the motivation of someone caught in the messiah trap , the messiah could give their last dime to the poor but not out of care but out of a key for escape, an escape to feeling everyone’s else pain that you don’t stop long enough to feel your own pain.

Steal Like An Artist , Austin Kleon

Steal Like An Artist offers a refreshing perspective on creativity, urging us to move beyond the fear of copying and instead embrace influence as a springboard for innovation. Kleon uses the famous quote by Wilson Mizner to illustrate this point: "if you copy from one author it's plagiarism but if you copy from a hundred it's research."

William W. Atkinson, The Power of Concentration

Atkinson suggests it's easier to concentrate on negative things. This resonates with modern psychology. Our brains are wired for negativity bias, a survival mechanism that kept us alert to threats. Dwelling on the negative can feel more stimulating than focusing on the positive Deep concentration on a challenging task can lead to greater accomplishment and learning than breezing through multiple simple ones

The ideas of Plato Republic

Polemarchus initially suggests that someone skilled in defending something must also be skilled in attacking it. A doctor skilled in treating disease could also create it, and a skilled guard could also be a skilled thief. This concept highlights the potential for expertise to be used for both good and bad purposes. He argues that appearances can be deceiving, and true friendship or enmity should be based on a person's character rather than our perception of their honesty or wickedness

The Rules of Wealth , Richard Templar

The Rules of Wealth, challenges our traditional understanding of value. He argues that a price tag doesn't always reflect the true worth of something The example of a £100 wine at a prestigious restaurant like La Gavroche perfectly illustrates this point. The price reflects not just the wine itself, but the entire dining experience – the ambiance, service, and exclusivity. The buyer is paying for value, not just the beverage

Anthony Robbins, MONEY Master the Game: 7 Simple Steps to Financial Freedom

Remember to take emotion out of investing because emotion is what so often destroys investing success, we all have money in our lives; what matters is you master money all you have to do to earn more money in the same amount of time is simply become more valuable
